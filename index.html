The Science of Food

Exploring the chemical and physical interactions in culinary systems through hands-on laboratory application.

"Studying Nutrition at UTSA goes beyond textbooks; it is about understanding what truly occurs when we cook. My journey through the Food Science Practicum has been pivotal in connecting scientific theory with the everyday act of food preparation."

My goal is clear: to master how culinary techniques impact the nutrients that ultimately nourish the body. Each lab has been an opportunity to witness chemical reactions in real-time, moving from abstract concepts to tangible understanding.
Lab 04

Quick Breads & Yeast Breads

Reason for Selection

This was one of my favorite labs as it clearly illustrated the critical difference between biological and chemical leavening. The challenge of precision—learning that altering the sequence of mixing steps can ruin the final product—was a valuable lesson in culinary discipline.

Dietetic Application

Understanding gluten formation is essential when counseling patients with Celiac disease. Seeing how buckwheat pancakes (dense texture, nutty flavor) differ from wheat products helps me provide realistic expectations for gluten-free alternatives.

Lab 05

Enzymatic Browning & Fruit

Key Takeaways

I gained a practical understanding of why fruit degrades and how to prevent it. We observed enzymatic browning in action (Polyphenol Oxidase) and how osmosis affects texture. The difference in cellular structure when cooking in water (hypotonic) versus syrup (hypertonic) was visually striking.

Dietetic Application

Teaching patients simple preservation techniques, like using citrus juice to prevent oxidation, can reduce food waste. Furthermore, understanding how to manipulate texture is vital for preparing palatable meals for patients with dysphagia or chewing difficulties.

Lab 06

Vegetable Cookery & Pigments

Reason for Selection

The priority here was nutrient retention. It became abundantly clear that steaming is the superior method for preserving vitamins, flavor, and appearance. The yogurt-based Alfredo sauce was a revelation—proving that creamy textures can be achieved without heavy fats if temperature is controlled to prevent curdling.

Dietetic Application

Many patients believe healthy food is bland. Demonstrating that vegetables can retain vibrant color and crunch through proper pH control and cooking methods empowers patients to enjoy plant-based diets rather than tolerating them.

Lab 07

Fats, Oils & Emulsions

Key Takeaways

The most impactful visual was watching donut holes absorb oil, reinforcing the concept of lipid absorption and smoke points. We analyzed why frying with butter is chemically inefficient compared to high-smoke-point oils. This lab emphasized that fat choice defines both flavor and chemical quality.

Dietetic Application

This translates directly to heart-healthy education. Understanding smoke points helps me advise patients on which oils to use for cooking versus finishing, reducing their intake of oxidized lipids and free radicals.

Lab 08

Egg Cookery & Substitutions

Reason for Selection

This lab transformed my understanding of protein coagulation. We compared traditional scrambled eggs against commercial substitutes (Egg Beaters), analyzing texture and nutritional profiles. The tofu brownie experiment was particularly successful, demonstrating functional substitution without compromising flavor.

Dietetic Application

I feel much more capable of guiding patients toward healthier options without them feeling restricted. Being able to suggest substitutions like tofu for eggs allows for cholesterol reduction while maintaining protein density.

Lab 10

Meat Cookery & Safety

Key Takeaways

Everything revolved around juiciness and safety. I confirmed that exceeding optimal temperatures causes muscle fibers to contract and expel water (syneresis), resulting in dry meat. We focused heavily on internal temperatures to ensure food safety standards were met.

Dietetic Application

Food safety is a non-negotiable aspect of dietetics. Beyond flavor, understanding the thermal death time of pathogens ensures I can provide safe cooking guidelines to vulnerable populations, such as the elderly or immunocompromised.

Lab 11

Legumes & Plant Proteins

Reason for Selection

Closing with legumes and tofu was impactful. We observed how hard water affects pectic substances, preventing beans from softening, and how salt/soaking can mitigate this. Recipes like tofu noodles and hummus demonstrated the versatility of plant proteins.

Dietetic Application

Combining legumes with grains to form complete proteins is a foundational concept. I walk away with practical, affordable menu ideas to offer patients who need high-fiber, low-cost nutrient density.
