Food Science Practicum

Scientific and practical aspects of food

"As a nutrition and health student at the University of Texas at San Antonio, I am committed to learning a solid foundation in the scientific and practical aspects of food."

Throughout my experiences in the Applied Food Science laboratory, I have learned the principles of food science as it relates directly to food preparation, nutrition education, and health. My goal as a student and future professional is to understand how food preparation and changes in nutrients influence health. Each laboratory has helped me analyze the chemical reactions and changes in food nutrients depending on how they are prepared, leading to a practical approach for better understanding.
Lab 04

Quick Breads & Yeast Breads

The Experience

One of my favorite laboratories was Lab 4: Quick Breads and Yeast Breads. In this lab, I learned to differentiate mixing processes and the importance of proper gluten development in products such as muffins, quick breads, and yeast breads. My favorite pancake variation was the buckwheat one, for its slightly dense texture and earthy, nutty flavor, enhanced by the s'mores-style topping.

Key Takeaways

The biggest challenge of the lab was following the steps precisely, as a small mistake could alter the final outcome. This lab strengthened my ability to connect cooking techniques with the scientific principles of food production.

Lab 05

Enzymatic Browning & Fruit Cookery

Scientific Observation

In this lab I learned how enzymatic browning occurs in fruits and why they change color after being cut.

Practical Application

I also observed how different cooking methods affect the texture and color of fruits and vegetables.

Lab 06

Vegetable Cookery

Cooking Methods

In this lab, I learned the importance of using cooking methods that better preserve the nutrients, color, and texture of vegetables. One of the methods we used was steaming, which is a very useful option because it prevents vegetables from losing water-soluble vitamins and better maintains their appearance and flavor.

Recipe Modification

One of the recipes we prepared was yogurt-based Alfredo pasta. I didn't know that such a good flavor could be obtained using yogurt instead of the traditional cream. I learned that yogurt can cause milk proteins to curdle if overheated, but in the recipe, a cooling step was used that prevented this. The final result had a smooth texture and a good balance of flavors.

Lab 07

Fats & Oils

Observations

The observations in this lab were about oil absorption and food texture, something evident with the donut holes. I also reinforced the importance of the smoke point of oils and why certain fats like butter are not suitable for frying.

Skill Development

This lab strengthened my skills in analyzing what happens chemically in the kitchen and how that influences food quality.

Lab 08

Egg Cookery

Comparisons

This lab helped me better understand the structure of the egg and how it changes over time and with different cooking methods. The comparison between scrambled eggs and Egg Beaters allowed me to analyze differences in color, texture, flavor, and nutrition, something important when making recommendations according to patient needs.

Substitutions

Trying substitutes like tofu in brownies showed me how certain ingredients can improve the nutritional profile without affecting quality. In general, this lab strengthened my ability to observe, compare, and apply food chemistry concepts, something I will use in the future to guide people seeking healthier or diet-adapted options.

Lab 10

Meat Cookery & Safety

Scientific Principles

In this lab, I learned how internal temperature and cooking method affect juice loss and the texture of ground meat. I observed that higher temperatures and longer cooking increase water loss, while fat content influences juiciness.

Safety Application

I also learned the importance of reaching the correct temperature for food safety. This lab taught me how to apply scientific principles to obtain safe, flavorful, and nutritious meats.

Lab 11

Legumes & Plant Proteins

Nutritional Benefits

In this lab, I learned about the benefits of legumes and tofu. I observed how minerals in hard water affect bean cooking and how techniques like soaking with salt help soften them. I also learned that legumes are high in fiber and protein, and that combining them with grains provides a complete protein.

Practical Application

In this lab, we also used different practical and nutritious recipes, such as tofu noodles and hummus.
